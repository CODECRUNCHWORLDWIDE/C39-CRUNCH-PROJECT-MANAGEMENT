# Week 4 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems, ~1.5 hours each. Unlike the exercises, these have **no single right answer** — the skill is judgment under real constraints, not recall. Do them after all three exercises.

1. **[Challenge 1 — Recalibrate inflated points](challenge-01-recalibrate-inflated-points.md)** — a team's story points have quietly drifted from what they used to mean, and their velocity chart is lying to everyone who reads it. Diagnose it and fix it. *(~90 min.)*
2. **[Challenge 2 — Plan to capacity](challenge-02-plan-to-capacity.md)** — take the real Sprint 3 and Sprint 4 capacity numbers and plan a two-sprint delivery against them, with a defensible commitment and stretch forecast for each sprint. *(~90 min.)*

## How these are judged

There's no answer key with one correct point value or one correct sprint plan. Instead, each challenge tells you what a *strong* submission looks like. You're being graded on:

- **Grounded reasoning** — every number you produce should trace back to a query against real data (history, capacity, reference class), not a gut call presented as if it were computed.
- **Explicit assumptions** — when the data underdetermines the answer (and it will), say what you assumed and why.
- **Honest uncertainty** — a submission that states a single confident number where the data supports a range is a weaker answer than one that states the range and explains it.

Keep your work in `challenge-01.md` / `challenge-02.md` with your queries **and** your written reasoning. The reasoning is the point.
