# Week 6 — Challenges

Two open-ended problems, no single right answer. Where the exercises checked recall and mechanics, these test judgment — the actual job of a PM under real, messy conditions.

## How to work these

- Each challenge is deliberately underspecified in places. That's not an oversight — deciding what's ambiguous and stating your interpretation *is* the skill being tested.
- Write your reasoning down, not just your final answer. A confident register with no stated assumptions is a weaker submission than one that shows its work.
- Both challenges expect real SQL and/or Python artifacts, not prose descriptions of what you'd build — this week's data-tooling rule applies here too.
- There's no answer key. Compare your reasoning against the rubric in each challenge, and against a classmate's or mentor's if you can.
