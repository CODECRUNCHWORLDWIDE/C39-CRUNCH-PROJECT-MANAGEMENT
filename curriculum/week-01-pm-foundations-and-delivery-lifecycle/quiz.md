## Answer key

<details>
<summary>Reveal after attempting</summary>

1. **B** — framing, unblocking, and honest communication, not task assignment or technical decision-making. (Lecture 1, §3)
2. **C** — Atlas has a defined outcome (Team Workspaces shipped) and an end (closure); Northlight Insights as a whole is the *product* it lives inside. (Lecture 1, §2)
3. **B** — no real end date and no real "done" is the defining tell of operations, regardless of budget or who assigned it. (Lecture 1, §2)
4. **C** — the tech lead owns *how* it gets built. The PM can push back if an approach seems to threaten commitments made elsewhere, but doesn't set technical direction. (Lecture 1, §4)
5. **B** — early, small bad news preserves trust and leaves time to actually react; late, large bad news is a trust failure, not just a scheduling one. (Lecture 1, §3c)
6. **B** — Initiation → Planning → Execution → Monitoring & Control → Closure. (Lecture 2, §1)
7. **B** — without a shared, written definition, everyone proceeds on different assumptions, confidently, and the result satisfies none of them. (Lecture 2, §2)
8. **A** — it was made visible and agreed, not silently absorbed — that's the whole distinction Lecture 2 draws between healthy scope management and scope creep. (Lecture 2, §4 and §7)
9. **B** — monitoring is the project's "nervous system," running continuously alongside execution, not a separate phase that waits for execution to finish. (Lecture 2, §5)
10. **B** — formal acceptance, clean handover, and a retrospective are exactly what's lost when closure is skipped; none of the other options describes what closure actually delivers. (Lecture 2, §6)
11. **C** — it has a number (30%), a timeframe (60 days), and is checkable by anyone with the adoption data — the other options are all unfalsifiable aspirations. (Lecture 3, §2)
12. **B** — success criteria measure the business outcome the project was funded to produce (often knowable only after launch); definition of done marks the project itself complete, independent of that outcome. (Lecture 3, §2)
13. **C** — the honest, healthy move is to make the trade-off explicit and let the sponsor choose with full information — not silently absorb it (eroding quality) or refuse the conversation. (Lecture 3, §3)
14. **B** — predictive fits when requirements are genuinely stable and the cost of a late/malformed outcome is severe — exactly this brief's shape. (Lecture 3, §4)
15. **B** — Agile fits because Elena expects to learn from real customer feedback and adjust priorities, and the team benefits from shipping incrementally rather than locking in every detail up front. (Lecture 3, §4)

</details>
