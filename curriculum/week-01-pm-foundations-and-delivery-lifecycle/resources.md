## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Project** | A temporary effort with a defined start, end, and outcome. |
| **Product** | An ongoing thing that's built, shipped, and evolved indefinitely — no fixed end. |
| **Operations** | Repeatable, continuous work with no defined "done." |
| **Sponsor** | The person who owns *why* a project exists and whether it's worth funding; approves the charter. |
| **Product Owner (PO)** | Owns *what* gets built and in what order; prioritizes the backlog. |
| **Tech lead** | Owns *how* a project gets built and whether an estimate/timeline is realistic. |
| **Project Manager (PM)** | Owns the plan staying true and everyone staying aligned to it, or to an explicit agreed change. |
| **Charter** | A one-page agreement on objective, scope, constraints, and success criteria, signed before work starts. |
| **Success criteria** | Measurable, checkable statements of what business outcome the project must produce. |
| **Definition of done** | The point at which the project itself — independent of business outcome — is considered complete. |
| **Scope (in/out)** | The explicit boundary of what a project will and will not deliver. |
| **Constraint** | A fixed, non-negotiable fact of the project (a date, a budget, a compliance requirement). |
| **Triple constraint** | Scope, time, and cost — moving one forces at least one of the others to move, or quality silently absorbs the pressure. |
| **Predictive (waterfall)** | A delivery approach that plans the whole project up front and executes the plan largely as written. |
| **Iterative** | A delivery approach that builds in repeated passes toward a known target, refining each time. |
| **Agile** | A delivery approach that builds and ships in short cycles, continuously re-prioritizing based on what's learned. |
| **Scope creep** | Unplanned, unapproved growth in a project's scope, usually happening silently during execution. |
| **Retrospective** | A closure-phase review of what went well and what to change next time. |
