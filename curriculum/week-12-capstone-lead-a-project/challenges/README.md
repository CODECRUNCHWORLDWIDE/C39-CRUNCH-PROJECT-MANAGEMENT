# Week 12 — Challenges

Two challenges. Both stretch beyond what the lectures walked you through step by step — the first gives you a realistic mid-delivery crisis to diagnose and respond to with your own data; the second asks you to write the single hardest document this course covers: an honest final report when the numbers aren't all good news.

## How to work these

- **Challenge 1** uses a provided dataset (not your own project's data) so every learner faces the identical, realistic slip scenario regardless of how their own capstone is actually going. Load the provided data into a scratch table, don't touch your real `capstone_items`.
- **Challenge 2** uses your **own** capstone data — the delivery report you write here is a stretch, deeper version of what Lecture 3, §5 and the mini-project ask for, meant to be done in addition to (not instead of) your real delivery report.

## What "stretch" means this week

Every challenge in this course asks you to go beyond the lecture's worked example. This week specifically, the stretch is **emotional as much as technical**: Challenge 1 asks you to recommend cutting scope or breaking a commitment using data, under simulated pressure from a sponsor who won't love the answer. Challenge 2 asks you to write about your own project's shortfalls as plainly as its wins. Neither is a coding exercise dressed up as hard — they're genuinely the harder half of the job.
