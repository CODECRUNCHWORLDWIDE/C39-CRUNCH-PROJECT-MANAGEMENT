## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Stakeholder** | Anyone whose support you need or whose opposition can hurt the project, whether or not they're on the delivery team. |
| **Power** | How much a stakeholder can affect the project's fate — budget, date, scope, or an outright kill decision. |
| **Interest** | How much a stakeholder actually cares about and pays attention to this project's outcome, right now. |
| **Power/interest grid** | A four-quadrant map (Manage Closely / Keep Satisfied / Keep Informed / Monitor) used to plan differentiated engagement. |
| **Sponsor** | The stakeholder who owns whether the project is worth continuing and what wins under the triple constraint. |
| **Silent blocker** | A stakeholder with high power and low current visible interest, at risk of reacting badly if surprised later. |
| **Communication plan** | A table specifying, per stakeholder, the message focus, channel, and cadence used to keep them engaged. |
| **RAG status** | Red/Amber/Green status reporting, trustworthy only when each color is defined against fixed, observable criteria. |
| **One-glance summary** | A short block (overall + sub-RAGs, headline, top risk, decision needed) that stands alone as a complete, honest answer to "how's it going." |
| **Escalation** | A structured request handing a decision to someone with the authority to make it — fact, impact, ask, and recommendation. |
| **Change request** | A logged, evaluated request to change scope, date, or budget — approved or rejected explicitly, never actioned silently. |
| **Underlying need** | The actual interest behind a stakeholder's stated position, often revealing options the stated position alone didn't. |
